The morning sun filtered through the sheer curtains, casting long shadows across the worn wooden floor. Eleanor stood at the window, watching the street below come to life with the familiar rhythm of the city awakening.

She had always loved this hour, when possibility hung in the air like morning mist. The coffee shop on the corner was just opening, its warm lights beckoning early risers with promises of fresh pastries and rich espresso.

Today would be different, she told herself. Today she would finally make the call she'd been putting off for weeks. The letter sat on her desk, its envelope worn soft from repeated handling.

But first, coffee. Everything seemed more manageable after coffee. She smiled at the thought, reaching for her coat.
